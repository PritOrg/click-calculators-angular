Complementary Angles

Complementary angles are two angles whose sum is 90 degrees (or π/2 radians). To find the complement of an angle, you can use the following formulas:

For Radians

To find the complement of an angle, say 'x', expressed in radians, use the formula:

Complement of xrad = π/2 - x

Example 1: The complementary angle of 1/3π radian = π/2 - 1/3π = π/6rad.

For Degrees

To find the complement of an angle, say 'y', expressed in degrees, use the formula:

Complement of y° = 90° - y

Example 2: The complementary angle of 30° = 90° - 30° = 60°.
